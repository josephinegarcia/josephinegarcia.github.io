Three Fun Exercises!

Improving fine motor control in young children can significantly improve their quality of life. There are several movement disorders that can affect young children, including ADHD, cerebral palsy, autism spectrum disorder, and hyperkinetic disorders such as tics, tremors, chorea, and dystonia. Some treatments and therapies for these include hand therapy for improved dexterity. Dex is a rehabilitation game that is fun and engaging, making physical therapy easier for children.

Exercise 1

This motion of sweeping the hand in an arc to flick away the shapes encourages the strengthening of the muscles that control wrist pronation, extension, and ulnar deviation. This exercise is targeted towards reclaiming wrist mobility in young patients with fine motor skill deficiencies.

Exercise 2

This motion of grabbing the shapes strengthen finger extension and flexion. This exercise is targeted towards improving finger dexterity.

Exercise 3

This motion of using the extended palm to flip over the shapes encourages wrist and finger flexion and extension. This exercise is targeted towards improving finger and wrist mobility.
